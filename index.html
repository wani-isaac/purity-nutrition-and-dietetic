what is ulcers

Ulcer is any open sore/ wounds found on skin, lining of organs or any internal surface of any tissue
Ulcers are of different types and named according to the parts they affect

  • stomach/ gastric ulcers (stomach)
  • Duodenal ulcers (duodenum)
  • Peptic ulcers ( small intestine, lower esophagus or stomach)
  • Mouth ulcers

causes of ulcers

Helicobacter pylori bacteria

, these bacteria damages epithelial lining of the stomach, intestine or duodenum and the mucous that protect the epithelial membrane from gastric acid.
They also produce ammonia which neutralizes the stomach acid preventing their death

Regular use of certain medications

such as the Non-Steroid Anti-Inflammatory Drugs for example ibuprofen and aspirin.

Gastric acid (hydrochloric acid produced by the stomach).

Signs and symptoms

  • Burning sensation in the stomach / heart burn.
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Bloating/ belching / feeling of fulness.
  • Fatty food intolerance due to decreases secretion of bile salt from pancreas.
  • Dyspepsia (indigestion) due to decreased secretion of pancreatic juice
  • Epigastric discomfort or pain (tenderness)
  • Haematesis / coffee ground vomit or vomiting blood.
  • Melaena (tarry black stool)
  • Trouble breathing

complications

internal bleeding

Low level bleeding can cause severe anaemia.
Large haemorrhage which can be life threatening

Perforation

Ulcers results to holes in the stomach which in turn causes the leakage of the stomach content into the peritoneum causing peritonitis and acute abdomen.
This is an emergency situation and requires immediate repair by surgery

Scarring and stricture

This results to narrowing of the pyloris known as pyloric stenosis. This presents with upper abdominal pain and distension, vomiting and nausea and acid reflux.

nutrition therapy

The goal of nutrition therapy in ulcers patient is to reduce the signs and symptoms of ulcers such as the abdominal discomfort / pain.
The major principle for the diet of an ulcer patient is to eliminate all foods that cause irritation to the sores of found in the lining of the affected part of the gastrointestinal tract.

Foods allowed.

Alkaline and foods rich in fiber and probiotics counters the high levels of acids in the stomach hence providing relieve from the effect of acids on the stomach walls.

  1. Watermelon.
  2. Cauliflower.
  3. Bananas.
  4. Nuts.
  5. Cucumber.
  6. Lettuce.
  7. Root vegetables like potatoes, beet root, carrots etc.
  8. Green vegetables like asparagus, broccoli, green beans.
  9. Whole grains such as oatmeal.

foods to avoid

the main foods to avoid includes those that causes irritation to the stomach, highly spicy foods, difficult to digest foods, acidic foods as below

  1. Citrus fruit.
  2. Spicy foods like mint, garlic, tomatoes e.t.c
  3. Alcohol.
  4. Acidic foods like pineapple.
  5. Fatty or greasy foods.
  6. Caffein
  7. Tea.
  8. Carbonated beverages.
  9. Raw vegetables or salads.
